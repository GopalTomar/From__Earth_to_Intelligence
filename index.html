Calculate LULC Area in CSV using Google Earth Engine

Problem/Context

Monitoring Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) change is essential for urban planning, agriculture management, and environmental conservation. However, extracting accurate area statistics from classified maps can be time-consuming.

Objective

Automate the calculation of LULC area by class and export results into a structured CSV file for further analysis.

Approach/Steps

  1. Import classified LULC map and feature collection (e.g., administrative boundaries).
  2. Use reduceRegion and reduceRegions functions in GEE to calculate class-wise pixel counts.
  3. Convert counts into area (hectares/sq.km).
  4. Export results as a CSV file to Google Drive.
🌍 Streamlined LULC analysis workflow

Soil Erosion Assessment in Bangladesh: A Critical Insight

Problem/Context

Soil erosion threatens agricultural productivity, water quality, and biodiversity in Bangladesh. Identifying erosion-prone regions is critical for sustainable land management.

Objective

Map annual soil loss rates (t/ha/year) across Bangladesh to detect vulnerable zones and prioritize conservation efforts.

Approach/Steps

  1. Integrate rainfall, soil type, slope, and land cover datasets in GIS/GEE.
  2. Apply the Revised Universal Soil Loss Equation (RUSLE) to estimate erosion risk.
  3. Generate soil loss maps highlighting high-risk agricultural regions.
  4. Validate with ground truth or literature-based erosion data.
🌱 Agricultural sustainability insights

Increasing urbanization has led to rising noise levels, negatively affecting mental and physical health. Predicting noise hotspots can help city planners design quieter urban environments.

Urban Air Quality Assessment: NDVI vs NO₂

Problem/Context

Air pollution, particularly nitrogen dioxide (NO₂), poses serious health risks. Vegetation, indicated by NDVI, plays a role in reducing pollution levels.

Objective

Analyze how vegetation density (NDVI) influences NO₂ concentrations in urban environments.

Approach/Steps

  1. Import Sentinel-5P NO₂ and Sentinel-2 NDVI data.
  2. Perform spatial correlation and regression analysis.
  3. Identify regions where vegetation significantly reduces NO₂.
  4. Map pollution mitigation by green cover.
🌳 Green infrastructure planning

Detecting Heatwaves using ERA5-Land Data in GEE

Problem/Context

Heatwaves are increasing due to climate change, affecting ecosystems and human health. Detecting their occurrence is vital for risk management.

Objective

Identify and map heatwaves globally using ERA5-Land monthly aggregated temperature data.

Approach/Steps

  1. Import ERA5-Land data in GEE.
  2. Calculate 95th percentile baseline temperature (2000–2025).
  3. Detect events exceeding the baseline between 2005–2010.
  4. Generate heatwave intensity and frequency maps.
⚡ Climate resilience planning

Satellite Image Change Detection for Deforestation

Problem/Context

Deforestation monitoring is crucial for biodiversity conservation and carbon management. Manual detection from satellite images is inefficient.

Objective

Automatically detect deforestation by analyzing changes in satellite imagery across time.

Approach/Steps

  1. Import multi-temporal Sentinel-2 or Landsat images.
  2. Apply pre-processing (cloud masking, normalization).
  3. Use change detection algorithms (NDVI differencing, deep learning).
  4. Highlight areas with significant vegetation loss.
🌲 Conservation automation
